Active Conditions Affecting Diet
Panhypopituitarism Craniopharyngioma NAFLD Diabetes Mellitus Hypothyroidism High Cholesterol Adrenal Insufficiency Diabetes Insipidus Hypogonadism
🫀
NAFLD + High Cholesterol
Avoid added sugar, trans fat, fried foods. Prioritise omega-3s (salmon, walnuts), soluble fibre (oats, beans), and olive oil.
🩸
Diabetes Mellitus
Low glycaemic index foods. Pair carbs with protein/fat. Consistent meal timing to avoid glucose spikes. Avoid sugary drinks.
🦋
Hypothyroidism (Secondary)
Take thyroid meds 30–60 min before eating. Limit raw cruciferous veg in excess. Ensure adequate selenium (Brazil nuts, eggs) and iodine.
🧂
Adrenal Insufficiency
Don't skip meals — risk of adrenal crisis. Slightly higher sodium needs. Take hydrocortisone with or just after food to avoid nausea.
💧
Diabetes Insipidus
Critical: monitor fluid intake/output. Avoid excessive water (risk of hyponatremia on DDAVP). Limit high-sodium processed foods.

⚠️
Alcohol
Worsens NAFLD, interferes with adrenal meds, disrupts sleep. Avoid entirely.
⚠️
Ultra-processed foods
Spike blood sugar, worsen liver fat, raise cholesterol. Replace with whole-food alternatives.
⚠️
Grapefruit / grapefruit juice
Interacts with many medications. Avoid unless cleared by prescriber.
⚠️
Skipping meals
With adrenal insufficiency, meal skipping can precipitate a crisis. Eat on schedule.
